Customized Bootstrap Portfolio

During my training at eMobilis, I customized a Bootstrap portfolio template to reflect my own identity, qualifications, and learning journey. I incorporated my own content and images while adapting the structure to suit my needs.

One of the most challenging aspects of the project was working with Jinja templating. I encountered errors spanning multiple pages and had to debug through large sections of unfamiliar code. The process was time-consuming and often frustrating, but it taught me the value of persistence and careful troubleshooting.

Although I have forgotten some of the finer details over time, the experience gave me confidence that I can understand and adapt existing codebases. It also reminded me that learning is iterative, and revisiting concepts is an important part of mastery.

Key Takeaways

  • Customizing existing systems requires both patience and curiosity.
  • Debugging is a skill developed through practice.
  • Knowledge fades without reinforcement, making refreshers essential for long-term growth.

Pride Sokoni Path(AI Safari Capstone)

A human-centered AI capstone project exploring how lending systems can better reflect the realities of informal workers, farmers, and small-scale traders.

Pride Sokoni Path was developed as part of my AI Safari capstone project. The project explored how technology could support more inclusive lending practices by recognizing that many economically active individuals remain excluded from traditional financial systems.

Credit systems often exclude economically active citizens because they were not designed around their realities. Women market vendors, rural borrowers, and smallholder farmers frequently lack formal employment records or established credit histories despite actively participating in local economies.

Through this project, I learned to approach problems with empathy before technology. Rather than assuming what users need, I learned to understand their daily routines, frustrations, and goals. This shifted my focus from building features to designing systems that support real people in meaningful ways.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy should guide innovation.
  • Technology should complement human judgment rather than replace it.
  • Effective solutions begin with understanding the people they are intended to serve.

Nexus Growth Folio

A cloud-deployed portfolio project that introduced me to static website hosting on AWS S3 and the real-world process of making applications publicly accessible.

Nexus Growth Folio was developed as part of my Cloud Fundamentals learning journey. The goal was to explore how a static website could move beyond a local development environment and become accessible through cloud deployment.

Using AI-assisted development tools, I created a portfolio website and prepared it for deployment. I then hosted the project using AWS S3 static website hosting, gaining hands-on experience with cloud services and understanding how web applications can be delivered to users.

This project shifted my perspective on cloud computing. Before this experience, cloud technologies seemed complex and intimidating. By working through the deployment process myself, I discovered that these tools become much easier to understand when applied to a real problem.

Key Takeaways

  • Deployment is an essential part of the development process.
  • AI can accelerate development when paired with human guidance and ownership.
  • Cloud technologies became less intimidating once I used them to solve a real problem.
